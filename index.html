Cyber Fraud Case: Kasongo Wamunyoro

Case Overview

Case Summary:

Kasongo Wamunyoro, a 31-year-old resident of Kent, Washington, was arrested on 15th May 2025 on charges of cyber fraud and identity theft. He was denied bail by the judge because of his flight risk, highlighting the severity of the crime committed.

Crime Details:

Kasongo used phishing techniques and malware to acquire stolen financial data, which he then used to commit multiple instances of wire fraud, transferring funds from victims' bank accounts to his own shell accounts. This deliberate crime involved sophisticated digital methods.

Legal Statutes:

  • Identity Theft: Washington State Law – RCW 9A.56.020 and RCW 9A. 9A.90 – Classified as a Felony.
  • Wire Fraud: Federal Crime – 18 U.S.C. §1343.

Policing: Arrest & Investigation

How Law Enforcement Tracked Kasongo:

  • The FBI cybercrime unit initiated an investigation after receiving multiple reports of fraudulent transactions linked by a unique digital footprint.
  • Advanced digital forensic analysis of server logs and cryptocurrency transactions led agents to Kasongo's IP address and online aliases.
  • A court-ordered warrant allowed for the monitoring of his online activities, ultimately pinpointing his physical location.

Details of Arrest:

  • Kasongo was arrested at his apartment in Kent, Washington, during an early morning raid conducted by a local patrol unit in coordination with federal agents.
  • During the arrest, agents discovered extensive evidence, including hard drives containing vast amounts of stolen financial data, direct access to hacked bank accounts, and several high-quality fake identification documents.
  • Forensic teams immediately began processing his digital devices for further evidence related to the crime.

Courts: Trial & Sentencing

Charges & Legal Process:
